Target Customer Personas

Persona A — WFH Professional

Age 25–38, works from laptop, visits 3–5×/week, spends RM 20–35 per visit. Values fast WiFi, good coffee, and a quiet seat. Loyalty-card user. Peak hours: 9am–12pm weekdays.

Persona B — Weekend Social Group

Age 22–32, comes with 2–4 friends, visits 1–2×/week, spends RM 15–25 per person. Values Instagram-worthy drinks, good food, relaxed atmosphere. Peak: Sat–Sun 10am–3pm.

Persona C — Neighbourhood Regular

Age 30–55, resident within 3km, visits daily, spends RM 8–15, usually takeaway. Values consistency, familiar staff, fast service. Peak: 7am–9am, 5pm–7pm.

Pricing Strategy

Use a Cost-Plus with Market Validation approach. Calculate your true cost, add a margin, then verify against local competitors.

StepFormulaExample (Latte)
1. Calculate COGSIngredients + Packaging + Utilities portionRM 2.80 + RM 0.40 + RM 0.30 = RM 3.50
2. Apply Target Cost %Target cost % for beverages: 25–28%RM 3.50 ÷ 0.27 = RM 12.96
3. Round & market validateCheck against 3 nearby competitorsRound to RM 13 (within RM 12–16 range)
4. Contribution Margin CheckSelling Price – COGSRM 13 – RM 3.50 = RM 9.50 per cup ✓
💡 Pricing Principle Price to value, not just cost. Ambience, quality, and location justify premium pricing. Underpricing devalues your brand and is very hard to reverse.

Layout Zones & Customer Flow

Zone Planning

  • Entry Zone: Clear sightline to menu board, cashier immediately visible, aroma draws customers in
  • Order & Pay: Single queue line, POS at shoulder height, menu board above and behind staff
  • Bar Zone: Espresso machine facing customers (theatre effect), waste chute below, milk fridge within arm's reach
  • Collection Point: Separate from order point, clear signage, napkins & condiments nearby
  • Seating: Mix of 2-top, 4-top, window bar seating. Power outlets at every third seat minimum
  • Restroom: Rear of space, never adjacent to food prep

Bar Workflow (Left to Right)

  • ① Grinder → ② Portafilter prep → ③ Espresso machine → ④ Milk steaming station → ⑤ Cup assembly
  • Barista should never cross paths mid-order
  • Cold brew / batch brew tap at end of bar
  • Ice machine under counter, accessible mid-bar
  • Blender station for frappes: away from espresso zone (noise + vibration)
  • Speed rail: most used syrups & powders at arm's reach
  • Cup station directly above bar, pre-warmed cups on machine

Halal Certification (JAKIM)

⚠️ Important Halal certification from JAKIM takes 3–6 months minimum. Apply immediately once your business is registered. Operating with "HALAL" signage without cert is a legal offence.
  • Apply via JAKIM's online portal: halal.gov.my
  • All ingredients must be from JAKIM-approved halal suppliers
  • Maintain halal ingredient log (purchase orders as proof)
  • Ensure no cross-contamination protocols in place
  • Non-halal items (alcohol, pork) must be completely absent
  • JAKIM audit: inspector visits unannounced — premises must always be compliant
  • Renewal: every 2 years. Display cert prominently at entrance.
  • During the waiting period, promote as "No pork, no lard, no alcohol" — do not use the HALAL logo until certified

Menu Engineering Matrix

⭐ Stars — High Profit + High Sales

Feature prominently on menu. Train staff to recommend. Never remove. E.g. Signature Latte, Flat White.

🐄 Cash Cows — Low Profit + High Sales

Bestsellers with thin margins. Reduce portion slightly or renegotiate supplier cost. E.g. black coffee, americano.

❓ Puzzles — High Profit + Low Sales

Good margin but nobody orders. Improve description, train staff to upsell, reposition on menu. Seasonal promotion candidate.

🐕 Dogs — Low Profit + Low Sales

Eliminate or replace within 2 menu review cycles. They confuse customers and drain kitchen complexity.

Par Level System

Par Level Formula
Par Level = (Daily Usage × Lead Time Days) + Safety Stock (3 days usage)

Example: Milk usage = 10L/day, lead time = 1 day, safety stock = 30L → Par = (10×1) + 30 = 40L

Standard Operating Procedures

10 core SOPs that every team member must know, follow, and enforce.

SOP 01 — Store Opening

SOP-01 · Store Opening · Time Required: 45 minutes · Responsible: Opening Shift Lead
1
Arrive 45 min before opening. Unlock, disarm alarm, turn on lights.
2
Temperature check: inspect fridge/cold room logs. Acceptable range: 0–4°C. Above 5°C → escalate immediately, do not serve perishables.
3
Turn on espresso machine. Allow 20 min warm-up. Do NOT pull shots until machine reaches operating temperature.
4
Flush groupheads (10 seconds each), backflush with water, check portafilter gaskets for wear.
5
Dial in espresso: pull 3 test shots, record dose/yield/time in dial-in log. Adjust grind until within spec (18g in / 36g out / 27–30 seconds).
6
Prepare condiment station: syrups, napkins, straws, stirrers, lids — fully stocked and clean.
7
Count & verify opening float (RM 300 standard). Sign cash reconciliation form. Place in cash drawer.
8
Log in to POS. Confirm menu prices, day, and date. Print zero report.
9
Kitchen: turn on exhaust, check gas supply, calibrate oven, prep mise en place per kitchen opening checklist.
10
Quick hygiene sweep: wipe counters, mop entrance, straighten furniture, check restrooms.
11
Brief team huddle (5 min): day's specials, menu unavailabilities, reservations, daily targets.
12
Flip open sign, unlock front door. Store is open.

SOP 02 — Store Closing

SOP-02 · Store Closing · Time Required: 60 minutes · Responsible: Closing Shift Lead
1
Last order call: 30 min before closing time. Inform all seated customers politely.
2
At closing: flip sign, lock front door, allow remaining customers to finish.
3
Espresso machine: backflush with Cafiza (3× chemical, 5× water rinse). Clean steam wands, drip trays, groupheads. Turn off machine last.
4
Grinder: purge remaining beans, brush burrs, wipe exterior. Record grind setting in log.
5
Bar: wash all jugs, pitchers, cups. Sanitise all surfaces. Clean syrup pumps.
6
Wrap and refrigerate all open food with date labels. Discard food past shelf life. Record waste log.
7
Cash reconciliation: count drawer → record → shift lead counts independently → both sign → manager verifies.
8
Mop all floors, clean restrooms, empty all bins (no bins left overnight). Replace bin liners.
9
Check all taps off. Turn off gas at main valve. Turn off kitchen equipment and exhaust.
10
Complete closing checklist (sign and date). Turn off lights (leave security light on), arm alarm, lock all doors.

SOP 03 — Cash Handling

SOP-03 · Cash Handling · Responsible: Cashier + Shift Lead · Reviewed: Manager
1
Opening float is RM 300. Count and verify by TWO people before shift begins. Both sign the reconciliation form.
2
Never leave cash drawer open unattended. Close after every transaction.
3
All sales must be rung through POS — no off-system transactions. Discounts require manager PIN.
4
Cash drop: when drawer exceeds RM 1,000, remove excess to safe. Document drop slip.
5
Closing count: cashier counts → records → shift lead counts independently → both sign → manager verifies.
6
Acceptable variance: ±RM 5. Over RM 10: manager investigation required and documented.
7
Bank deposit: cash deposited within 24 hours. Deposit slip filed with daily records.

SOP 04 — Espresso Preparation

SOP-04 · Espresso Preparation · Standard: 18g in / 36g out / 27–30 sec · Responsible: Barista
1
Purge grouphead: 3-second flush before each shot to clear old water and stabilise temperature.
2
Dose: grind 18g (±0.1g) into portafilter. Verify with scale.
3
Distribute: use distributor tool or Stockfleth's move to level the bed. No mounding in the centre.
4
Tamp: 15–20kg pressure, perfectly level. Wipe portafilter rim clean.
5
Lock portafilter and start extraction immediately. Target: 36g out in 27–30 seconds. Finer = slower, coarser = faster.
6
Taste every batch (every 2 hours or after grind adjustment). Record in dial-in log.
7
Failed shots (sour/bitter/blond): discard, re-pull. Never serve a shot you wouldn't drink yourself.

SOP 05 — Customer Service

SOP-05 · Customer Service · The GREET-KNOW-SERVE-THANK Framework
G
Greet within 30 seconds of customer entering. Eye contact, warm smile, "Selamat datang! / Welcome to [Shop Name]!"
R
Read the customer. Are they regulars? Rushing? Browsing? Adjust your pace and approach accordingly.
E
Engage & Recommend. Know the menu. Offer one genuine recommendation per table.
E
Enter the order accurately. Repeat back order before proceeding. Confirm customisations (milk type, sweetness, temperature, size).
T
Time the delivery. Beverages within 5 min, food within 15 min. Proactively update if delayed.
K
Know your regulars. Remember names, usual orders, preferences. This builds loyalty more than any loyalty card.
S
Serve with care. Place drinks with handle toward customer. Announce the drink clearly.
T
Thank every customer by name when possible upon departure. "Thank you — see you next time!"

SOP 06 — Complaint Handling (LAST Method)

SOP-06 · Complaint Handling · Escalation within 5 minutes
L
Listen. Do not interrupt. Let the customer fully express the issue. Body language: calm, not defensive.
A
Acknowledge & Apologise. "I completely understand your frustration, and I sincerely apologise." — Never say "that's our policy."
S
Solve. For quality issues: offer to remake. For wrong order: correct immediately at no charge. Major complaints: involve manager.

Thank. "Thank you for bringing this to our attention — it helps us improve." Log the complaint with date, issue, and action taken.

SOP 07 — Inventory SOP

SOP-07 · Inventory · Weekly Count: Sunday Close · Monthly Full Count: Last Sunday of Month
1
Print Inventory Count Sheet from POS. Assign one counter per section (bar, dry goods, kitchen, packaging).
2
Count physically — do NOT estimate. Weigh coffee beans. Count bottles to the unit. Measure bulk items in kg/litre.
3
Compare to system quantity. Note variance (±10% = investigate source).
4
Update POS inventory to match physical count. Flag any items below par level for immediate reorder.
5
Check all date labels. FIFO rotation: new stock goes behind old stock on every shelf.

SOP 08 — Food Safety SOP

SOP-08 · Food Safety · MOH Guidelines & HACCP Principles
1
Temperature danger zone: 4°C – 60°C. Food must not remain in this range for more than 2 hours.
2
All refrigerated items labelled: product name, open date, expiry date, prepared-by initials.
3
Cross-contamination: colour-coded cutting boards (green = veg, red = protein, white = dairy/bread). Never reuse without sanitising.
4
Receiving: check delivery temperature, inspect for damage, verify halal certs. Reject any item above threshold or with damaged packaging.
5
Food safety incident: isolate affected food, inform manager, document, report to MOH if illness suspected.

Cash Flow Management

⚠️ Cash Flow Rule #1 Revenue in the bank ≠ profit. Track actual cash — not just sales. Many profitable businesses fail because of cash flow timing.
  • Maintain minimum RM 20,000 cash buffer at all times (3 weeks operating cost)
  • Collect daily cash sales — deposit within 24 hours. No "slush fund" in the drawer
  • Track all payables: when rent, salaries, and supplier invoices are due — calendar them
  • Set up a separate bank account for: (1) Operations, (2) Tax reserve, (3) Maintenance reserve
  • Monthly: move 5% of gross revenue to maintenance reserve, 10% to tax reserve
  • Never mix personal and business finances — ever
  • Review accounts monthly with your accountant

Risk Register

RiskLikelihoodImpactPreventionResponse Plan
Espresso Machine Breakdown Medium Very High Daily cleaning, quarterly service, service contract Call supplier immediately. Activate backup menu. Consider mobile machine rental. Inform customers.

Customer Food Safety Complaint Low Very High Strict HACCP compliance, daily temp logs, food handler training, pest control contract Isolate product. Remove from service. LAST method. If illness suspected: notify MOH, preserve evidence.
Power Failure Medium High Keep TNB emergency number. UPS for POS. Know main switch location. Switch to manual order-taking. Check cold storage (max 4 hrs before food risk). Contact TNB. Communicate on social media.
Low Sales / Slow Period Medium High Strong marketing pipeline, loyal customer base, delivery channel, seasonal promotions Analyse root cause. Activate promotion campaign. Reduce labour hours proportionally. Explore B2B / catering revenue.
Negative Social Media / Reviews Medium Medium Excellent service culture, proactive review solicitation, Google monitoring alerts Respond within 24 hrs, professionally and empathetically. Take conversation offline. Investigate internally. Never argue publicly.

First 90 Days — Week by Week

A detailed action plan for the critical first 12 weeks of operation.

Phase 1 — Launch & Stabilise (Weeks 1–4)

Week 1 — Soft Opening
Operations: Implement all SOPs. Track every issue. Speed will be slow; that's normal.
Marketing: Open to friends & family + 3 influencers. Share behind-the-scenes content.
Finance: Daily sales tracking begins. Establish baseline ATV and cover count.
Feedback: Paper feedback forms for every table. Read ALL feedback same night.
Week 2 — Fix Issues
Operations: Address Top 5 issues from Week 1 feedback. Speed improvement target: all drinks under 7 min.
Marketing: Announce soft opening officially on social. Start daily posting routine.
Finance: First COGS calculation. Are we on track for 30% or under?
Week 3 — Grand Opening
Operations: Grand opening event. Full team present. Emergency contact list ready.
Marketing: Influencer posts go live, giveaway activates, first Google Business post.
Finance: Set up formal daily sales report. Owner reviews every day end of Week 3.
Week 4 — Operational Rhythm
Operations: First full stock count. Compare to POS. Identify waste sources.
Finance: First week of payroll complete. Statutory submissions (EPF, SOCSO) for Month 1.
KPIs: Review ATV, daily covers, Google rating, beverage cost %. Set targets for Month 2.

Phase 2 — Grow & Optimise (Weeks 5–8)

Week 5 — Menu Optimisation
Run first menu engineering analysis. Identify dogs — consider removing. Promote stars more aggressively. End of Month 1 P&L — review actual vs. projected.
Week 6 — Delivery Platform Launch
Register on Grab Food / Foodpanda. Design delivery-optimised packaging. Train team on delivery order handling. Target: 20 Google reviews this month.
Week 7 — Community Event
First local partnership activation. Host a Latte Art Class or Coffee Tasting evening — 15–20 seats at RM 60/pax. Excellent content + bonding + revenue.
Week 8 — 2-Month Review
Full operational audit: SOPs followed? Equipment serviced? End of Month 2 P&L — are we profitable? If yes: celebrate and plan reinvestment. If no: analyse and cut costs.

Phase 3 — Systemise & Scale (Weeks 9–12)

Week 9 — Systems Tightening
Review all SOPs — update based on 2 months of learning. Plan seasonal menu. Identify top performer for title upgrade and added responsibility.
Week 10 — Loyalty & Retention
Analyse loyalty card data: who are your top 20% customers? VIP customer appreciation event — exclusive preview of new seasonal menu.
Week 11 — B2B Growth
Identify 5 corporate clients within 1km. Approach with corporate coffee package proposal. Tax review with accountant: SST registration due?
Week 12 — 90-Day Strategic Review
Full 90-day P&L vs. projections. All KPIs vs. Month 1 baseline. Formal 3-month staff performance reviews. Owner strategic review: next milestones — second outlet? renovation upgrade? new equipment?
